## The read (tape → why no entry at the close)

- The 6/18 candle is a textbook **blow-off / distribution wick** at a new ATH on heavy volume (pitfall 27 §4). Buying ¥11,745 means being the last FOMO buyer.

## Entry plan — the retest ladder (buy the HOLD, not the touch)

Three Schelling-point zones, laddered (~⅓ each), each entered **only on a volume-contraction hold + reclaim / inside-day** — not the first touch:

| Zone | Level | Confluence | vs 6/18 close |
|---|---|---|---|
| ① shallow (probe) | **¥11,100–11,150** | prior high 11,125 + bottom of the 6/18 gap (11,095) | −5% |
| ② core | **¥10,000–10,320** | 6/16 gap + EMA9 (10,159) + ¥10k round number | −12 to −15% |
| ③ deep (best R:R) | **¥9,000–9,200** | EMA21 (9,159) + 6/09–12 congestion shelf | −22% |
| ✕ invalidation | **high-volume close < ¥9,000** | parabola consensus broken → stand aside, wait for a *new* base; do **not** average toward SMA50 ¥6,828 (regime change, not a retest) | — |

**Expression**: cash-secured short put at ¥10,000 / ¥9,000 (zones ②/③) — get paid to be the bid at the level; assignment builds the long-hold at your price. Premium / IV must be read in **IBKR** (OSE chain), not TV / Funda.

## What this case demonstrates (lessons)

1. **Pitfall 27 in the field**: the entry is the *confirmed hold* at a Schelling-point retest — not the price touch, and not the blow-off close.
2. **Quantify extension before waiting "for a pullback to a key MA"**: on a +187%-over-200dma parabola the nearest MA is a −20%+ event; the word "pullback" hides the size of the dip.
3. **Honest data limits**: with OSE flow unavailable, the distribution read rests on the candle alone — a **yellow flag, not a confirmed top.** Confirm the 6/18 catalyst (a real AI-MLCC re-rating vs pure momentum — price-action P7) and the Murata fiscal-Q1 earnings date (~late July) before selling a CSP into possibly-low IV.
