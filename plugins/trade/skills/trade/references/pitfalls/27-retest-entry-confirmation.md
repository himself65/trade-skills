## A pullback entry is the confirmed HOLD, not the touch — a retest probes WHO holds the level

Waiting for a pullback before entering a trending name is correct — but the signal is **not price *touching* a reference level, it is the level *holding* under stress.** The reference level (a key moving average, a prior swing high, a gap edge) carries no information by itself; what carries information is **who shows up to bid when price returns to it.** A retest is a stress test that flushes weak hands (FOMO chasers) and reveals committed holders (diamond hands). Buy the confirmed hold, not the first touch — and never chase a blow-off candle, where you would be the last FOMO buyer.

**Why it matters**: This is the *execution layer* of the price-action framework — P8 (float composition determines stress behavior: a high price held only by FOMO is brittle), P5 (a pullback is holder rotation; the question is *who sells and who bids*), P4 (a retest holds when the vacuum zone above is intact and committed bids defend the level). The recurring, expensive error is treating a **high price as strength** and chasing it, instead of waiting for the stress test that reveals holder quality. It is the rigorous form of the trader's aphorism *"adversity reveals who actually holds — anyone can click buy in an uptrend."*

**How to apply**:

### 1. A pullback is a retest of a Schelling-point reference, not an indicator

The level "works" only because **enough participants watch it** → resting orders cluster there → it becomes where committed bids actually congregate. The MA has no intrinsic magic; it is a coordination point. This is the rigorous content of "technical analysis is psychology." A retest can be against **any** of these (it is a family, not one indicator):

- the crowd-watched moving average (20 / 50 / 200-day SMA, or a 9 / 21 / 50 EMA cluster)
- a prior swing high / breakout level — **polarity flip**: old resistance → new support
- an unfilled gap edge
- anchored VWAP from a key event
- a trendline / channel boundary
- a round number
- a volume-profile POC / value-area edge

**Confirmation is volume**: a pullback into the zone on **contracting** volume that holds = strong-hand base, no eager sellers → valid entry. A **high-volume** break = consensus has moved lower → invalidation: flip (pitfall 4), don't bottom-fish (price-action P6).

### 2. Buy the hold, not the touch

Entry trigger = price reaches the zone and **then** prints either (a) a down-day or two *into* it on contracting volume followed by a reclaim / inside-day, or (b) an intraday rejection wick that holds the close. The first touch is not the signal; the hold is. **Ladder** bids across the 2–3 nearest reference zones; do not full-size at the shallowest (the shallowest touch applies the least stress and is the most likely to fail).

### 3. On an extended/parabolic name, the nearest real support can be −15 to −25% — quantify it FIRST

After a parabolic run the watched MAs lag far below price, so **waiting "for a pullback to a key MA" can mean a 15–25%+ drawdown, not a shallow dip.** Before deciding which retest is realistic, compute the **extension**: close vs EMA9 / EMA21 / SMA50 / SMA200, plus recent ATR as a % of price. If the nearest meaningful support is >15% away, the "dip you're waiting for" is a real correction — **pre-size for it and pre-place the ladder**, or you will either chase the top or panic into the flush. A move all the way to a far MA (e.g. the 50-day at −40%) is **not a retest** — it is a regime change / parabola collapse; don't average into it as a "dip," wait for a *new* base to form (price-action P6).

### 4. A blow-off / long-upper-wick at a new high on heavy volume is exhaustion, not an entry

Signature: a gap-up or thrust to a **new high that closes in the bottom ~quartile of the day's range**, an upper wick on the order of one ATR, on above-average volume. That is distribution / FOMO exhaustion — the people buying the high are the last FOMO buyers. It is a **"wait and read the pullback" signal, not a "short it" signal** — one candle is not a confirmed top (price-action P5: a pullback is holder rotation; the *hold-or-fail* read on the retest is what decides direction).

### 5. Mechanical checklist

1. Mark the 2–3 nearest Schelling-point zones (prior high / breakout, the crowd's MA / EMA cluster, the unfilled gap).
2. Quantify extension (close vs EMA9 / EMA21 / SMA50 / SMA200) and ATR%. Know how far each retest is and how violent the tape is.
3. Do **not** buy the touch — require a volume-contraction hold + reclaim / inside-day, or an intraday rejection-hold close.
4. Ladder (~⅓ per zone). Never full-size the shallowest zone.
5. Invalidation = a high-volume close below the deepest *trend-intact* reference (the structural shelf / EMA cluster) → abort; don't average toward the next MA down (regime change, not a retest). Flip per pitfall 4.
6. Express the bid as a **cash-secured short put** at / below the reference strike — get paid to be the bid; assignment builds the position at your price (subject to options availability — non-US listings may need a broker like IBKR, not TV / Funda).
7. Don't chase a blow-off wick — the entry is the subsequent retest-hold, not the close.
